    • Geographic financial performance: Implications for local real estate prices and REIT stock returns


      Abstract: We posit that quarterly profitability changes of firms headquartered within a geographic region manifest in regional real estate valuation implications. Indeed, regional profitability indices constructed from real-time quarterly reports provide timely information about future real estate values, especially in tight local real estate markets. However, this profitability-housing link is not fully processed in REIT prices exposed to regional real estate values. Overall, quarterly profitability analyzed at the geographic level has substantial economic implications. Regional profitability is relevant for timely understanding of local real estate markets and their consequences and, thus, it is a primitive factor affecting the macroeconomy.


      by Scott Joslin (USC Marshall), and Yaniv Konchitchki (UC Berkeley Haas).
